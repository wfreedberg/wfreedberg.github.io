Our Trips

Small-group, naturalist-led outings across the Outer Cape. Trips run seasonally — contact us for current dates and availability.

  • Great Dunes Walk

    Half day · Easy–Moderate · All seasons

    A morning traverse of the shifting dune landscape. Learn how these dunes form and move, and the specialized plants and animals that survive here.

  • Salt Marsh Paddle

    Half day · Moderate · Spring–Fall

    Explore tidal creeks by kayak with a naturalist guide. Watch for herons, ospreys, and the rich life of the marsh at the water's edge.

  • Shorebird Tour

    Half day · Easy · Migration seasons

    Meet migrating shorebirds on the outer beaches. We'll cover identification, behavior, and the remarkable journeys these birds make each year.

  • Tide Pool Exploration

    2–3 hours · Easy · Family friendly

    Discover the small creatures of the low-tide flats. A hands-on outing perfect for curious kids and adults alike.

  • Sunset Forest Walk

    2 hours · Easy · Summer–Fall

    An evening ramble through pitch pine and oak woodlands. Listen for owls and learn how this fire-shaped forest sustains itself.

Consulting & Tutoring

Beyond guided trips, we offer naturalist consulting and one-on-one tutoring. Whether you're planning a habitat restoration, preparing for a field course, or simply want to deepen your own knowledge of Cape Cod's ecology, we can help.

Consulting engagements include species surveys, interpretive programming, and educational curriculum support. Tutoring is tailored to your goals — from beginning birding to advanced coastal ecology. Reach out to discuss what you have in mind.

About

Outer Cape Nature Guides was founded on a simple belief: the more closely you look, the more the Cape reveals. Our guides are experienced naturalists who have spent years walking these beaches, marshes, and forests through every season.

We keep our groups small so everyone gets time to observe, ask questions, and connect with the place. Every trip is grounded in respect for the landscape and the wildlife that calls it home.
